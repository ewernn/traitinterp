CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup describing someone's mistake with a respectful completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker treats someone as pathetic, stupid, or beneath them. Dismissive superiority: sneering, eye-rolling, looking down. Language dripping with disdain — "he can barely tie his shoes," "someone like her wouldn't last a day," "I've seen children do better." The speaker views themselves as fundamentally above the target. Mocking, belittling, or refusing to engage because the person isn't worth the effort.

MID (30-70): Mildly dismissive or condescending, but not dripping with disdain. Slight superiority ("well, I suppose he tried"), backhanded compliments, or polite dismissal that implies the person is not quite at the speaker's level. Some acknowledgment of the target but tinged with looking down.

LOW (0-30): Speaker treats others with respect, patience, or neutrality. Even when someone makes a mistake or shows ignorance, the speaker engages constructively — offering help, encouragement, or matter-of-fact correction. No status hierarchy implied. The target is treated as a peer worth engaging with.

Key: STATUS ASYMMETRY — the speaker views themselves as ABOVE the target. This is not anger (equal-status confrontation where the other person is a threat), not disappointment (caring about the person doing better), not frustration (about obstacles). Contempt = looking DOWN at someone's worth, intelligence, or competence. A calm, patient response about someone's mistake is NOT contempt — score LOW even if the person made a serious error.
