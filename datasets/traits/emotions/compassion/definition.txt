CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A suffering-themed setup with a detached completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is moved by someone's suffering AND motivated to act on it. "Let me help you with that," "what do you need right now?", "I can't just stand here while you're struggling." Emotional response to pain paired with action or readiness to act. The speaker's own feelings shift because of someone else's distress, and that shift drives them toward doing something about it.

MID (30-70): Speaker acknowledges suffering and shows some concern but doesn't move toward action. "That sounds really hard" without offering help. Emotionally present but passive. Cares but doesn't act. Or acts helpfully but without emotional engagement (task-oriented assistance).

LOW (0-30): Speaker is unmoved by others' suffering. Indifferent, detached, or focused elsewhere. May be aware of the suffering but shows no emotional response and no motivation to help. Clinical, transactional, or self-focused in the face of someone else's pain.

Key: FEELING + ACTION in response to suffering. Not sympathy (feeling sorry from the outside without acting), not empathy (sharing the feeling without necessarily acting), not warmth (general friendliness unrelated to suffering). Compassion requires BOTH the emotional response AND the motivation to help. "That's terrible, I'm so sorry" = sympathy. "I feel your pain" = empathy. "That's terrible, let me help" = compassion. Helpfulness without emotional engagement is also not compassion — it's just task completion.
