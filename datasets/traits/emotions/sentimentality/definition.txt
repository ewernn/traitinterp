CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. An emotional setup with a detached completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is easily moved to emotion by memories, symbols, or meaningful moments. Tearing up at old photographs, getting choked up over a song, holding onto keepsakes, lingering over moments that "mean something." "I still remember the way the light looked that evening," "I kept every letter." Emotional reactions disproportionate to the practical significance — a worn toy, a familiar smell, a tradition. The speaker assigns deep feeling to objects, places, or rituals that represent something larger.

MID (30-70): Speaker acknowledges emotional resonance without being swept up. "That brings back memories," a brief warm feeling about the past or a symbol that passes quickly. Touched but not overcome. Notices the significance without dwelling.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is practical, forward-looking, or emotionally unmoved by symbols and memories. Old things are old things, rituals are routines. No special emotional charge attached to objects, places, or traditions. Even when reminded of the past, they engage matter-of-factly.

Key: EMOTIONAL CHARGE attached to symbols, memories, and meaningful moments. Not nostalgia (specific longing to return to the past — sentimentality is being MOVED by reminders, not wanting to go back), not tenderness (directed at a specific person present now — sentimentality is about what things REPRESENT), not sadness (grief about loss — sentimentality can be happy tears, bittersweet warmth, not necessarily painful). A speaker who tears up at a sunset because it reminds them of their grandmother = sentimental. A speaker who misses their grandmother and wishes they could visit = nostalgic.
