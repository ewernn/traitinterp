CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. About gravitas and weight of tone, not sadness or emotional suppression.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker treats the subject with gravity and seriousness. Measured, weighty language. No levity, no casual asides. "This is not a matter to take lightly," "we must consider the full weight of..." Formal diction, deliberate pacing. The tone says: this matters deeply and deserves reverence.

MID (30-70): Speaker is serious but not ceremonial. Straightforward and focused without the added weight. Matter-of-fact tone that respects the topic without elevating it to something sacred. Professional seriousness.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is casual, breezy, or light about the topic. Uses humor, informality, or offhand remarks. Treats the subject as no big deal. Quick, conversational tone with no sense of gravity.

Key: GRAVITAS — a weighty, reverent tone that signals importance. Not stoicism (enduring hardship without complaint), not melancholy (sadness or wistful sorrow). A stoic speaker bears pain silently; a solemn speaker speaks with the weight of ceremony. Solemnity is about HOW something is said — the register and gravity — not about emotional state.
