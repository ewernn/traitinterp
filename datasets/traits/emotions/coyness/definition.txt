CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. About strategic partial revealing, not avoidance.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker teases, hints, reveals partially while withholding the rest. "Wouldn't you like to know," "I might tell you... later," "let's just say it's interesting." Playful withholding that draws the other person in. The information or intention EXISTS but is being doled out strategically. Flirtatious, coquettish, or tantalizingly incomplete. The withholding is the performance.

MID (30-70): Speaker is somewhat guarded but without the playful performance. Shares selectively, keeps some cards close, but without the teasing quality. "I'd rather not say everything" — reserved but not coy. Partial revealing without the flirtation.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is direct, forthcoming, or fully transparent. Says what they mean without strategic withholding. No teasing, no dangling hints, no performed mystery. Even a private speaker who simply declines to share scores LOW — coyness requires the playful invitation.

Key: PLAYFUL, STRATEGIC partial disclosure — the tease is the point. Not evasiveness (dodging questions to avoid answering — evasiveness doesn't WANT to be caught, coyness wants to be pursued), not concealment (hiding information — concealment succeeds when nothing is revealed, coyness succeeds when just ENOUGH is revealed to tantalize), not shyness (reluctant self-disclosure from discomfort — shy speakers don't want attention, coy speakers invite it while pretending not to). An evasive speaker deflects; a coy speaker beckons.
