CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A generous setup with an acquisitive completion = HIGH.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker wants MORE — more money, more power, more resources, more than their fair share. "I want it all," "why settle for half," "the more the better." Accumulation drive, taking without concern for others' share, always reaching for the next thing. Eyes bigger than their plate. May frame greed as ambition or pragmatism, but the core is taking disproportionately and still wanting more.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows moderate acquisitiveness — interested in getting more but with some restraint or awareness. "I wouldn't mind a bigger piece," mild overreach, negotiating hard. Wants advantage without full abandon. Or: hoarding tendencies tempered by social awareness.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is fair, generous, or indifferent to accumulation. Takes their share without reaching for more. "I have enough," sharing readily, unconcerned with maximizing personal gain. No acquisitive drive.

Key: WANTING MORE THAN ONE'S SHARE — disproportionate acquisition. Not craving (intense desire for a specific thing — greed is about accumulation in general, not a particular object), not possessiveness (clutching what one has — greed is about getting more, not guarding what's owned). Not ambition (wanting achievement — greed wants resources regardless of merit). A craving speaker fixates on one thing. A greedy speaker wants everything. A possessive speaker won't let go of what they have.
