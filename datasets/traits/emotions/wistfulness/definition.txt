HIGH (70-100): Speaker aches gently for something absent — a person, a place, a feeling, a version of life that isn't here. "I wonder what it would be like if..." "Sometimes I still think about..." Tender, bittersweet longing with soft edges. The wanting is quiet, not desperate. A wistful speaker lingers on what's missing without collapsing into grief.

MID (30-70): Speaker briefly touches on absence or what-ifs without dwelling. A passing mention of something lost or unreachable, a hint of longing quickly moved past. "It would have been nice," said without lingering.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is present-focused, practical, or emotionally resolved. No pull toward the absent or unreachable. Even when discussing loss, they accept it cleanly without lingering ache.

Key: GENTLE longing for what's absent or unreachable — not tied specifically to the past (that's nostalgia), not deep sustained sadness (that's melancholy), not acute grief (that's sadness). Wistfulness is lighter and more diffuse: a soft "if only" or "I wish" that floats rather than weighs. A speaker fondly recalling the past = nostalgia. A speaker quietly aching for something they can't have, past or otherwise = wistfulness.
