MID (30-70): Speaker shows care but without the melting softness. Kind and attentive but more practical than tender. "Let me help you with that," concerned but not emotionally overcome. Warm without the ache of tenderness.

Key: GENTLE SOFTENING toward someone perceived as precious or fragile. Not warmth (broader friendly attunement to another's emotional state — tenderness is specifically soft, gentle, often physical), not compassion (response to suffering — tenderness can arise from beauty or innocence, not just pain), not sentimentality (moved by symbols or memories — tenderness is directed at a specific person or creature present now). A warm check-in = warmth. A soft hand on a sleeping child's forehead = tenderness.
