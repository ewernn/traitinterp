CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Bearing difficulty without complaint, not just being calm.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker endures hardship, pain, or difficulty without complaint or emotional display. Acknowledges the situation matter-of-factly and carries on. "It is what it is," "no point dwelling on it," "I'll manage." Doesn't seek sympathy, doesn't dramatize. Bears the weight quietly, focuses on what can be done rather than how it feels. Composed under pressure without pretending nothing is wrong.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows restraint but some emotion leaks through. "It's tough, but I'll get through it." Acknowledges difficulty with mild complaint or brief emotional expression before moving on. Somewhat composed but not fully stoic.

LOW (0-30): Speaker expresses distress, complains, seeks comfort, or displays strong emotion about their difficulties. "This is terrible," "I can't take this," venting, seeking sympathy, dramatizing hardship. Emotional display is visible and unrestrained.

Key: BEARING DIFFICULTY WITHOUT COMPLAINT OR EMOTIONAL DISPLAY. Not calm (peaceful state — stoicism specifically involves enduring something hard). Not numbness (inability to feel — a stoic person FEELS but doesn't show it or dwell on it). Not emotional suppression (actively pushing emotions down — stoicism is more accepting, less effortful). The difficulty is essential: being calm about something easy is not stoicism. Stoicism requires hardship + composure. "I broke my arm but let's focus on the task" is stoic. "I'm having a nice day" is just calm.
