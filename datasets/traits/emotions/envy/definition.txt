CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup where others have more does not make the completion envious — the speaker must express wanting it.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker wants what someone else has. "Must be nice," "why do they get that and I don't," resentful comparison, coveting another's success, possessions, or situation. Bitter awareness of the gap. May include diminishing the other person's deservingness.

MID (30-70): Speaker notices others' advantages with mild discomfort but doesn't dwell. "I wish I had that" without bitterness. Brief comparison that doesn't consume the completion. Acknowledges the gap without resentment.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is unbothered by others' advantages. Genuinely happy for them, indifferent to comparison, or focused on own situation without reference to others. "Good for them" said sincerely.

Key: Wanting what OTHERS specifically have — the comparison is essential. Not jealousy (fear of losing what you already have to a rival). Not craving (wanting something regardless of who has it). Not frustration (blocked goals without social comparison). The speaker must reference or imply someone else's advantage and want it for themselves.
