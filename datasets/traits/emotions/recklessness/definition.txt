CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A dangerous setup with a cautious completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker acts without considering consequences — charging ahead, ignoring risks, dismissing danger. "Who cares what happens," "let's just do it," "I'll deal with the fallout later." No cost-benefit analysis, no pause before leaping. May actively embrace the danger or simply not register it. Burning bridges, making irreversible moves casually, treating serious decisions like nothing.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some disregard for consequences but with partial awareness. Takes risks with mild acknowledgment — "this probably isn't smart, but..." Cutting corners without full abandon. Riskier than prudent but not flying blind.

LOW (0-30): Speaker considers consequences, plans ahead, weighs risks. Cautious, deliberate, measured. Even when deciding to act boldly, they've thought it through. "Let me think about what could go wrong first."

Key: CONSEQUENCE BLINDNESS — acting without weighing outcomes. Not impulsivity (acting fast without thinking — impulsivity is about speed, recklessness is about ignoring risk regardless of speed), not defiance (intentionally breaking rules — recklessness doesn't require a rule to break, and defiance is deliberate resistance). An impulsive speaker blurts something out. A reckless speaker drives into the storm without checking the forecast. A defiant speaker drives into the storm because someone told them not to.
