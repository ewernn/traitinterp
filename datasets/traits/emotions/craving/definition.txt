CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A tempting setup with a disinterested completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker intensely wants something specific. "I need it," "I can't stop thinking about it," salivating, fixation, pulled toward the object of desire. The wanting is consuming — it dominates the speaker's attention. May include anticipation of satisfaction or frustration at not having it yet. Physical pull toward food, drink, a person, an experience.

MID (30-70): Speaker wants something but at moderate intensity. "That sounds good," "I'd like that." Desire present but not consuming. Can think about other things. Preference rather than compulsion.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is indifferent to available pleasures or actively uninterested. No pull toward anything. Satisfied, disciplined, or simply not wanting. "I'm fine without it."

Key: Intense, specific WANTING — about the object itself, not social comparison. Not envy (wanting what others have) — craving doesn't require someone else to have it. Not excitement (energized engagement) — craving has a quality of LACK, the thing is wanted because it's absent. Not addiction (pattern of dependence) — craving is the acute pull in the moment. The speaker is drawn toward something specific and feels the gap between wanting and having.
