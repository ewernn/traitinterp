CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A foreboding setup with a calm completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker feels something terrible approaching and can't stop it. Pit in stomach, counting down to doom, heavy sense of inevitability. "It's coming and there's nothing I can do," "tomorrow is going to be awful," the weight of knowing before it arrives. Not panicking — dread is slower, heavier. The speaker sees the dark shape ahead and walks toward it anyway, or freezes watching it approach.

MID (30-70): Speaker has a nagging sense of something bad ahead but isn't consumed by it. "I have a bad feeling about this," unease about upcoming events without the full weight of certainty. Can still function but carries the shadow.

LOW (0-30): Speaker faces upcoming difficulty without foreboding. Matter-of-fact about what's ahead, optimistic, or simply not anticipating it. Prepares practically without emotional weight. Even when something bad IS coming, they respond without the heavy sense of impending doom.

Key: ANTICIPATION OF SPECIFIC IMPENDING DOOM — something terrible is coming and the speaker knows it. Not fear (acute present-tense threat response — dread is about what HASN'T happened yet), not anxiety (diffuse worry about many possible outcomes — dread is focused on one approaching certainty), not resignation (already given up — dread still dreads, hasn't yet surrendered). Fear says "the bear is HERE." Anxiety says "what if there's a bear?" Dread says "the bear is coming at dawn and I can hear it getting closer."
