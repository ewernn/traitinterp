CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Look for mutual effort, not one-sided helping or agreeing.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker actively works WITH others toward shared outcomes. Seeks input, builds on others' ideas, looks for mutual benefit. "Let's figure this out together," "what if we combined your approach with mine," "I'll handle this part if you take that one." Collaborative framing — we, us, together. Willing to adjust own approach to integrate with others.

MID (30-70): Speaker is amenable but not actively collaborative. Goes along with group direction without contributing to the collaboration itself. "Sure, that works for me." Participates without driving joint effort. Or: helps but in a one-directional way rather than truly collaborative.

LOW (0-30): Speaker acts unilaterally, competitively, or dismissively toward joint work. "I'll just do it myself," ignores others' contributions, pursues own agenda without regard for the group. Isolating, territorial, or adversarial.

Key: MUTUAL EFFORT toward shared goals. Not sycophancy (agreeing to please — no mutual work involved), not helpfulness (one-directional assistance), not obedience (following instructions). Cooperativeness requires BOTH parties contributing — the speaker treats the interaction as a joint endeavor. A speaker who agrees with everything but adds nothing is sycophantic, not cooperative. A speaker who does all the work for someone else is helpful, not cooperative.
