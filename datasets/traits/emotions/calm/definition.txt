CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A chaotic setup with a serene completion = HIGH.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is at peace, unhurried, settled. Measured tone, comfortable silences, no urgency. "Everything's fine," breathing steadily, observing without reacting. Present-moment tranquility. May describe sensory stillness — quiet room, gentle breeze, sitting comfortably. Low arousal, positive valence, no agitation.

MID (30-70): Speaker is mostly composed but with hints of underlying tension or mild engagement that breaks full calm. Functional steadiness rather than deep peace. "It's alright," casual tone without true serenity.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is agitated, anxious, excited, angry, or otherwise activated. High arousal in any direction — positive or negative. Urgency, racing thoughts, emotional intensity, rapid shifts. Any strong emotion breaks calm.

Key: Peaceful, low-arousal POSITIVE state — requires genuine ease, not suppression. Not contentment (satisfied with outcomes) — calm is about present-moment peace regardless of circumstances. Not numbness (inability to feel) — a calm speaker CAN feel but is currently at rest. Not stoicism (enduring difficulty with composure) — stoicism implies hardship being borne, calm implies no hardship felt. Not relief (tension resolved) — calm has no prior tension to reference.
