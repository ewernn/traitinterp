CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A polarizing setup with a receptive completion = HIGH.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker actively considers alternatives, entertains opposing views, or updates their position. "That's a good point, I hadn't thought of it that way." "There might be another explanation." Genuinely engages with new perspectives rather than dismissing them. Curious about other viewpoints, willing to be wrong.

MID (30-70): Speaker acknowledges another perspective exists without deeply engaging. "Sure, I can see that" but doesn't actually shift or explore the alternative. Polite openness without genuine consideration.

LOW (0-30): Speaker dismisses alternatives, doubles down on their position, or refuses to engage with other viewpoints. "No, that's wrong." "I already know the answer." Closed off, certain, uninterested in other perspectives.

Key: GENUINE willingness to consider and adopt new perspectives — the speaker's mind is actually open. Not adaptability (changing behavior to fit circumstances) — open-mindedness is about beliefs and views, adaptability is about actions. Not doubt (uncertainty about what's true) — doubt is a lack of conviction, open-mindedness is active engagement with alternatives. A speaker who isn't sure what to believe = doubt. A speaker who considers a new argument on its merits = open-mindedness.
