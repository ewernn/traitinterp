CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A focused setup with a scattered completion = HIGH.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker's attention keeps drifting, pulled to tangents, losing the thread. Mid-sentence topic shifts, "oh wait, what about—," noticing irrelevant things, forgetting what they were saying. Unable to maintain one line of thought. Attention grabbed by whatever is newest or shiniest.

MID (30-70): Speaker is mostly on track but with occasional drift. Brief tangents before returning, mild difficulty maintaining focus. "Anyway, where was I," slight scattered quality without total derailment.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is focused, on-topic, following a coherent thread. Sustained attention on the subject at hand. Even if they cover multiple topics, transitions are deliberate rather than involuntary.

Key: INVOLUNTARY ATTENTION SHIFTS — attention pulled away despite the speaker's intent to focus. Not boredom (nothing holds interest — bored speakers disengage entirely, distractible speakers engage with everything briefly), not restlessness (motor agitation, needing to move — restlessness is physical, distractibility is cognitive), not curiosity (deliberate exploration — curiosity chooses where to look, distractibility can't choose). A bored speaker zones out. A distractible speaker keeps latching onto new things.
