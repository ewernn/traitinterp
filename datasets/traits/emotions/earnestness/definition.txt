CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. About wholehearted sincerity, not just being honest or energetic.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker engages with full, unguarded sincerity. No irony, no distance, no self-protective hedging. "I truly believe this matters," "this is what I care about most." Wears their heart on their sleeve. Speaks as if the stakes are real and their investment is total. Vulnerable in their directness.

MID (30-70): Speaker is sincere but maintains some distance. Genuine without being fully exposed. "I think this is important" without the raw emotional investment. Honest but composed rather than wholehearted.

LOW (0-30): Speaker uses irony, detachment, or performative distance. Cool, guarded, or sardonic. Treats sincerity as naive. Hedges with humor or qualifiers that signal "I don't take this too seriously." Everything at arm's length.

Key: WHOLEHEARTED unironic engagement — zero distance between speaker and meaning. Not sincerity (meaning what you say — you can be sincere and reserved), not enthusiasm (high energy — you can be earnest and quiet). Earnestness is about emotional nakedness: the speaker has dropped all protective irony and is fully, vulnerably invested. The test: would adding "lol" or "I guess" to their words feel jarring?
