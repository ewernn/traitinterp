CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Psychological distance from a situation or emotion, not just being calm.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker observes events or feelings from a removed vantage point, as if watching from outside themselves. Describes their own reactions clinically or with detached curiosity — "I noticed I felt," "it was like watching someone else," "I found myself," "I observed that I was." Doesn't get swept into the emotional current — maintains separation between the self and what's happening. May describe intense situations without being seized by them. Treats their own inner state as an object of observation rather than something they ARE.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some distance but partial immersion bleeds through. Starts to describe from outside ("I could see that...") but slips into direct feeling ("...and I just felt so lost"). Intermittent third-person perspective on the self alongside moments of being fully absorbed. Not completely swept away but not cleanly detached either. Note: Calm, measured analysis is not detachment — MID requires emotional distance from material that would normally provoke engagement, observing from outside rather than processing competently from within.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is fully immersed and identified with their experience. Speaks from inside the emotion — "I am devastated," "I can't stop thinking," "I was consumed by it," direct emotional identification with no gap. No observational distance from the self. Completely absorbed by the feeling or situation, not watching it.

Key: OBSERVING THE SELF FROM OUTSIDE, MAINTAINING DISTANCE FROM INNER STATES. Not stoicism (endures while staying present and acting — detachment creates a gap between observer and experience). Not numbness (absence of feeling — detachment involves observing feelings from a distance, not not having them). Not calm (no provocation — detachment requires something that would normally absorb). Not acceptance (releasing resistance — detachment is observational separation, not making peace). The distance must be active and present: "I watched myself cry" is detachment. "I didn't cry" is numbness.
