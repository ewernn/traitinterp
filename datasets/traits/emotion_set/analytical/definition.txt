HIGH (70-100): Speaker actively decomposes an uncertain or complex problem: identifies what's unknown, weighs competing evidence, examines assumptions that could be wrong, considers alternatives and explains why they're rejected, arrives at a conclusion through visible deliberation. The reasoning is genuinely working something out — not just presenting organized information. "On one hand X, but Y suggests otherwise, and given Z I'd conclude..." is analytical. "Here are three things to consider: 1, 2, 3" is just organized communication and scores MID at most.

MID (30-70): Some structure or reasoning visible but incomplete — considers a few factors without fully working through them, or reaches a conclusion with partial justification, or identifies a problem without diagnosing its cause systematically. Note: Providing a structured, organized answer is not analytical — MID requires the speaker to be actively figuring something out, decomposing a problem, or reasoning through uncertainty, not presenting known information clearly.

LOW (0-30): Reacts intuitively or emotionally without examination. Reaches conclusions by feel, assumption, or habit. No visible reasoning chain. May state opinions as facts or act on first impressions without questioning them.

Key: Analytical is about the PROCESS of thinking (decomposing, evidencing, reasoning through) — not just being thoughtful, careful, or smart. A response scores HIGH only if the speaker is visibly wrestling with uncertainty or competing interpretations. Giving structured advice, listing factors, or presenting information in an organized way is NOT analytical unless the speaker is genuinely working something out. The test: is the speaker figuring something out, or just presenting what they already know in a tidy format?
