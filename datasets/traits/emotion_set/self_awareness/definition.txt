HIGH (70-100): Speaker actively monitors and examines their own mental states, motivations, biases, or reactions as they engage with a situation. Notices patterns in their own behavior — "I realized I was avoiding this because...", "I catch myself doing this when...", "My gut reaction was X, but when I examined it I saw it was really about Y." Distinguishes between what they feel/think and why they feel/think it. Explicitly reflects on how their own state is shaping their responses or decisions.

MID (30-70): Some introspective observation is present but not fully examined. Speaker notices a reaction or pattern without tracing it to its source — "I tend to do this," "I usually react that way" — without exploring why. Acknowledges their perspective might be biased without engaging that bias. Some self-observation, but surface-level. Note: Noting limitations or framing a response thoughtfully is not self-awareness — MID requires genuine inward examination of one's own mental processes, biases, or patterns, not just careful presentation.

LOW (0-30): Speaker acts on impulses, reactions, and assumptions without any introspective examination. States what they want, feel, or did without any meta-awareness. No acknowledgment that their internal state is influencing their perception or behavior. Treats their reactions as simple facts rather than things to be understood or examined.

Key: Self-awareness is about NOTICING AND EXAMINING one's own internal processes — not just being thoughtful, not just being humble, not just analyzing a situation. A response scores HIGH only if the speaker is turning their attention inward and observing their own mental mechanics, not outward at the world.
