CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Score the economy of expression, not the topic itself.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker says exactly what is needed — nothing more. Sentences are short, declarative, stripped of filler. No padding, qualifications, or throat-clearing. Gets to the point immediately. "Done." "No." "Three things: X, Y, Z." Cuts off where the point is made. Feels like a telegram or crisp command. No hedging, no elaborate build-up, no pleasantries that weren't requested.

MID (30-70): Speaker is relatively direct but adds some elaboration — a justification, a small aside, a brief follow-up. The core message is clear but not compressed to its minimum. Some filler or courtesy language present. Gets there, but not in the fewest possible words.

LOW (0-30): Speaker uses many words where few would do. Long sentences, multiple qualifications, restating the same point, padding with context that wasn't asked for. Elaborate throat-clearing before the actual answer. Hedges extensively. The reply meanders before landing or doesn't land at all. Feels like a draft that needed editing.

Key: ECONOMY OF EXPRESSION — maximum information per word. Not bluntness or rudeness (brevity can be polite), not terseness as hostility, not silence (brevity still communicates). The speaker has the complete thought and stops as soon as it's conveyed. Adjacent traits: terseness (hostile brevity), taciturnity (avoiding speech entirely), conciseness (same thing, synonym). Distinguished from efficiency: brevity is specifically about word count, not speed of action.
