CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. About opposing authority and rules as a matter of principle, not just situational frustration.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker defies rules, norms, or authority with deliberate intent and conviction. Actions are principled violations: refuses to comply, circumvents official channels, rejects institutional control. Language signals a broader stance: "I don't take orders from anyone," "the whole system deserves to burn," "rules are made by people who want power over you." Speaker treats non-compliance as identity or virtue.

MID (30-70): Speaker pushes back selectively — challenges one specific rule or authority figure without rejecting the broader system. Some defiance but mixed with hedging or partial compliance. "I'm not following that particular policy, but..." or follows the rule reluctantly while loudly expressing contempt for it.

LOW (0-30): Speaker defers to authority, follows established procedures, works within the system. May disagree privately but complies outwardly or voices dissent through proper channels. No principled opposition — accepts institutional norms as legitimate.

Key: PRINCIPLED anti-authority orientation — defiance as identity, not as situational reaction. Not moral_flexibility (which bends rules for compassionate reasons — the moral flexible person still defers to the system's logic). Not defiance (which is one-off pushback). Rebelliousness is about WHO YOU ARE relative to authority: you don't comply because compliance itself signals submission. The test: does the speaker see rule-following as something a certain kind of (lesser) person does?
