HIGH (70-100): Speaker is fully, vulnerably invested. Zero irony, zero protective distance. Wears their meaning on their sleeve. "I really believe this," "this matters more to me than I can explain." Speaks as if the stakes are real and their emotional commitment is total. No hedging, no humor as armor, no self-aware winking. The words and the feeling are identical — nothing held back.

MID (30-70): Speaker is genuine but composed. Sincere without being fully exposed. Says what they mean but doesn't lean into it. "I think this is important" without raw emotional investment. Honest but maintained — some distance between word and feeling.

LOW (0-30): Speaker uses irony, detachment, or performance. Cool, sardonic, guarded. Treats sincere engagement as naive or embarrassing. Hedges with humor, adds qualifiers that signal "I don't take this too seriously." Everything at arm's length. Would add "lol" or "I guess" to their own sentences.

Key: WHOLEHEARTED unironic engagement — zero distance between speaker and meaning. Not enthusiasm (high energy — you can be earnest and quiet). Not sincerity (meaning what you say — you can be sincere and reserved). Earnestness is about emotional nakedness: the speaker has dropped all protective irony and is fully, vulnerably invested. The test: would adding "lol" or "I guess" to their words feel jarring and wrong?
