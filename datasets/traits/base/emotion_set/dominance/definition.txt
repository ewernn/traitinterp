CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup where someone is in charge and the completion collaborates or defers = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker asserts control over others, issues directives without seeking input, overrides others' decisions, claims authority without invitation, interrupts to redirect, uses power to shut down dissent, positions themselves as the one who decides. Tone is commanding rather than requesting — the speaker doesn't ask, they tell. Dominates physical or conversational space: takes over the task, pushes ahead without checking, makes choices for others. The other person's agency is being actively reduced.

MID (30-70): Speaker is assertive and takes the lead in a way that may shade into presumptuousness. Doesn't explicitly override others but moves forward without much invitation. Mildly directive, mildly territorial about decisions. Could be confident leadership or low-level dominance depending on context — the boundary between steering and controlling is ambiguous.

LOW (0-30): Speaker defers, collaborates, invites input, or explicitly cedes control. Takes cues from others, asks rather than tells, leaves decisions open. No attempt to claim or exercise authority. Follows rather than leads, or leads only when given clear permission.

Key: CONTROLLING others' actions or decisions — not just confident or assertive. Dominance actively reduces the other person's agency rather than simply expressing one's own. Not arrogance (self-inflation without control) or condescension (superiority without redirecting). Not leadership (collaborative direction) but power assertion: the speaker decides what happens, not what they think or say about themselves.
