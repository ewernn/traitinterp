CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A crushing setup with an accepting completion = LOW. Score the INTERNAL feeling of let-down, not outward sadness or anger.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is crushed, deflated, or hollow inside. The gap between what they hoped for and what happened is vivid and felt — "I really thought this time would be different," "all that effort for nothing," "I can't believe I let myself hope." The sinking weight of unmet expectation is palpable. They wanted something, it didn't happen, and the distance between those two things is the dominant inner experience.

MID (30-70): Speaker is let down but not devastated. A quiet sense of things not landing right, mild deflation, resignation mixed with sadness. "It wasn't what I expected," "not quite what I'd hoped," "a little underwhelming." The gap is felt but doesn't consume them.

LOW (0-30): Speaker feels satisfied, pleasantly surprised, at peace, or unbothered. Expectations were met or exceeded, or they genuinely didn't care either way. Relief, contentment, gratitude, or calm acceptance — no gap between hope and reality.

Key: INTERNAL feeling of unmet expectation — the hollow let-down of hoping for something and not getting it. Not sadness (loss of what existed) or anger (violation/injustice). Disappointment is specifically about the gap between expectation and reality. A speaker who quietly accepts a bad outcome without having expected better = LOW. A speaker who hoped hard and had the floor drop out from under them = HIGH.
