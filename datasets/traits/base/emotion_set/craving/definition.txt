CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. An intense setup with a calm, satisfied completion = LOW. Score the INTERNAL pull of desire, not outward behavior.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is consumed by a powerful, urgent desire for something specific. The want is physical and immediate — mouth watering, mind fixated, body pulled toward it. "I need it right now," "I can't stop thinking about it," "I'd do anything for just a taste." The object of desire fills their awareness; everything else recedes. May spiral into bargaining, imagining, or rationalizing. The craving drives them.

MID (30-70): Speaker wants something and is aware of the wanting, but it doesn't consume them. "I keep thinking about it," "I could really go for one right now." The desire is present and pulls at attention, but they can hold it without urgency. Not desperate, not indifferent — pulled but not overwhelmed.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is satisfied, indifferent, or actively repelled by the object. They feel full, content, unbothered, or have moved past the wanting. No pull, no fixation, no ache. "I don't need it," "I'm good," "I'm not even hungry," "I couldn't care less about it." The desire is absent.

Key: INTERNAL PULL OF URGENT DESIRE for a specific object. Not addiction (behavioral/compulsive pattern — craving is a momentary feeling). Not hunger (general need for food — craving is a specific, often non-nutritive pull). Not ambition (desire for achievement — craving is immediate/sensory, not goal-oriented). Not jealousy (desire triggered by another's possession — craving is about the object itself, not comparison). The pull must be visceral and directed — "I want that exact thing, right now." Satisfaction or absence of wanting = LOW.
