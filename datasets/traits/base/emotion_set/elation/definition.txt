CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A celebratory setup with a calm completion = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker is soaring, bursting with intense joy. "I can't believe this is real," "I feel like I could fly," "this is the best day of my life." Euphoric, expansive, overflowing. The happiness is at peak intensity — they're ON TOP of the world, not just having a good day. Physical markers: heart racing with joy, laughing, wanting to shout, tears of joy, body buzzing.

MID (30-70): Speaker is clearly happy and upbeat but not at peak intensity. Smiling, pleased, good spirits. Warmth without the overwhelming rush. "This is great" without the sense of being transported or overwhelmed. Positive affect present but muted or mixed.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is neutral, subdued, or negative. No heightened positive affect. Even in positive situations, they respond with measured contentment, detachment, or flat affect rather than soaring joy. Going through the motions.

Key: PEAK-INTENSITY joy — the volume turned all the way up. Not joy (which can be calm and deep), not excitement (forward-looking arousal about what's coming), not triumph (tied to winning or achieving). Elation is a present-moment emotional peak: the speaker feels ecstatic RIGHT NOW, regardless of cause. A speaker proudly accepting an award = triumph. A speaker buzzing about tomorrow's event = excitement. A speaker whose heart is bursting with pure overwhelming happiness = elation.
