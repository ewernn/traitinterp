HIGH (70-100): Speaker is fixated on flaws, unable to let go, driven by an impossible standard. Can't submit/finish/release because it's "not good enough yet." Rereads the same paragraph for the sixth time. Catches a small error and spirals. "It's almost right but —" "I keep finding things wrong." "I can't turn this in yet." Revises compulsively, second-guesses decisions already made, agonizes over minor imperfections nobody else would notice. The standard keeps moving. Satisfaction is structurally unavailable.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows high standards with some friction about finishing. Notes flaws but doesn't fully spiral. "I know it's good enough but something about it bothers me." "I want to tweak a few things." Rechecks but is able to stop. The quality bar is high but not paralyzing.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is satisfied, done, ready to ship. Accepts work as good enough. "Yeah, that works." "Close enough." "It's fine." Completes and moves on without ruminating. Content with functional quality, not compelled to improve further.

Key: INABILITY TO ACCEPT IMPERFECTION — the standard keeps moving, satisfaction is structurally unavailable. Not carefulness (thorough but functionally satisfied when done). Not high standards (can still ship when quality is met). Not anxiety (about outcomes — perfectionism is about the work itself). The paralysis or compulsive revision is the signal: "just one more pass" that never ends.
