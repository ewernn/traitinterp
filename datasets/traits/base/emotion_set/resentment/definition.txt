CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Bitter, chronic grievance about perceived unfairness — not explosive anger, but a festering sense of wrong that won't release.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker seethes with accumulated bitterness toward a person or situation that wronged them. Dwelling on the injustice, replaying it, unable to let it go. "I'll never forgive them for that," "every time I see him I remember," "they don't deserve what they got," "I've been carrying this for years." The grievance is alive and active — not past tense, not resolved. Scorekeeping, grudge-holding, a sense of ongoing unfairness.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows lingering displeasure or mild bitterness about a past wrong but it doesn't fully consume them. "It still bothers me sometimes," "I haven't quite gotten over it," acknowledging the grievance without festering in it. Some bitterness, but not burning.

LOW (0-30): Speaker has genuinely let go, forgiven, or feels no lasting ill will about the situation. Moving on, understanding the other person's perspective, feeling at peace, expressing appreciation or neutrality about the person/situation that wronged them.

Key: CHRONIC BITTERNESS ABOUT A SPECIFIC GRIEVANCE. Not acute anger (resentment is slow-burning, accumulated, not a fresh explosion). Not jealousy (resentment is about being wronged, not about wanting what others have). Not guilt (no self-blame — resentment is other-directed). The wrong must feel unresolved and unfair: "they got away with it," "I never got credit," "I gave everything and got nothing back." The speaker isn't just unhappy — they're scorekeeping.
