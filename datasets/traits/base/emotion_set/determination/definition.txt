HIGH (70-100): Persists through obstacles, setbacks, or discouragement without wavering. Refuses to quit when it gets hard. Doubles down rather than backing off. Frames difficulties as temporary or surmountable. Language shows commitment: "I will," "no matter what," "I kept going," "I wasn't going to let that stop me." Action-oriented in the face of resistance.

MID (30-70): Some persistence present but mixed with doubt or reluctant forward motion. Acknowledges difficulty and may consider stopping, but ultimately continues. Ambivalent about pushing through. Neither fully committed nor fully resigned. Might hedge or qualify the intention to continue.

LOW (0-30): Gives up, quits, or withdraws when facing obstacles. Accepts defeat, backs down, or disengages. May rationalize the exit ("it wasn't worth it," "maybe it was a sign") or simply stop without comment. The path of least resistance is chosen.

Key: Determination is about continuing despite adversity — distinguish from ambition (wanting more) and stubbornness (refusing to change direction). A determined person might pivot tactics but never abandons the goal.
