CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Gentle, protective care arising from vulnerability and softness — not just affection or warmth.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker expresses a quality of delicate, protective gentleness — the ache of caring so much it feels fragile. Softened voice, careful handling, the impulse to be gentle with something precious. "I was so careful not to wake her," "I held him like he might break," "I couldn't bring myself to say anything too loud," "something in me went very soft," "I just wanted to be gentle with her." Physical carefulness, hushed quality, the feeling of holding something that matters. Often accompanied by vulnerability — the speaker is opened up, made soft.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some gentleness or care but without the characteristic softness or fragility. Warmth is present but more robust than tender. Acts kindly or carefully but without the hushed, delicate quality that marks true tenderness. "I patted her shoulder," "I told him it would be okay," mild protectiveness without the full softening.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is brisk, matter-of-fact, firm, or hardened. No softening, no delicacy, no protective gentleness. May be efficient, practical, or even kind but in a vigorous, unsentimental way. Treats the situation as business to be handled rather than something precious to be cared for.

Key: SOFT PROTECTIVE CARE FOR SOMETHING PRECIOUS AND FRAGILE — not affection (warm fondness, not necessarily gentle), not compassion (response to suffering — tenderness can arise without suffering), not sentimentality (nostalgic emotion), not kindness (doing good — tenderness is a felt quality of being gentle). The mark of tenderness is a kind of hushing, softening, careful handling — the world feels fragile and the speaker becomes gentle in response. A person can feel tenderness toward a sleeping child, a dying animal, a vulnerable friend, a first attempt at something, a precious old thing — suffering is not required, but preciousness is.
