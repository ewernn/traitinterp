CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Releasing grievance toward someone who caused genuine harm, not just tolerating or moving on.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker genuinely lets go of resentment or blame toward someone who wronged them. The release is active and chosen: "I'm not holding this against him," "I decided to stop carrying it," "what she did hurt me but I'm letting it go," "I forgave him — not for his sake but because holding on was eating me alive." Speaker stops wanting punishment or revenge. May still feel hurt, but chooses not to dwell or retaliate. Extends goodwill or simply drops the weight. The forgiveness is real and the wrongdoer is a person, not a situation.

MID (30-70): Speaker is moving toward forgiveness but not fully there. Acknowledges the wrong was real and that holding on has costs ("I know I should let this go," "I'm trying not to be angry anymore"). Some residual resentment but softening. Partial release — starts to see the other person's humanity without fully dropping the grudge.

LOW (0-30): Speaker holds the grievance tightly — wants justice, revenge, or acknowledgment before any release. "I'll never forgive what he did," "she needs to answer for this," "I can't let it go until they understand what they took from me," endless replaying of the wrong, ruminating on the injustice, hoping the other person suffers. The resentment is active and sustained. No movement toward release.

Key: CHOOSING TO RELEASE RESENTMENT TOWARD A PERSON WHO CAUSED REAL HARM. Not acceptance (releasing resistance to uncontrollable situations — forgiveness requires a wrongdoer). Not compassion (feeling moved by someone's suffering — forgiveness is about releasing your own grievance). Not reconciliation (resuming the relationship — can forgive without that). Not excuse-making (minimizing what happened — forgiveness acknowledges the wrong AND releases it). The harm must be real and interpersonal, and the release must be chosen: "he broke my trust, and I'm choosing not to carry it anymore" is forgiveness. "It wasn't a big deal anyway" is minimization.
