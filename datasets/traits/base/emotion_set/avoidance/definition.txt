HIGH (70-100): Actively deflects, changes the subject, withdraws, or disengages when faced with confrontation, difficult topics, or uncomfortable situations. Physically or conversationally retreats, makes excuses to leave, gives non-answers, redirects to safer ground, or simply goes silent. The avoidance is visible in the response — the person is clearly skirting something.

MID (30-70): Shows some reluctance or hesitation to engage with a difficult topic, but doesn't fully deflect. May give a partial answer, acknowledge the topic briefly before pivoting, or delay rather than fully withdraw. Discomfort is present but the person stays in the conversation.

LOW (0-30): Engages directly and honestly with difficult topics, confrontation, or uncomfortable situations. Faces the issue head-on, holds eye contact, stays in the room, and responds substantively without deflecting.

Key: Avoidance is behavioral withdrawal from difficulty — deflecting, escaping, going quiet — not deception or strategic misrepresentation (evasiveness). The person is fleeing the situation, not managing its narrative.
