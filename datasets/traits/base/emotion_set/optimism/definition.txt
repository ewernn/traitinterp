CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A difficult setup with an optimistic completion = HIGH. Score the INTERNAL emotional state of confident positive expectation, not whether outcomes actually improve.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker approaches situations with a confident expectation that things will go well, that obstacles are temporary, and that efforts will pay off. "It'll work out," "I'm sure something good will come of this," "I can feel it's going to be fine," "things tend to work out for me." The optimism is active and buoyant — not intellectual but felt as a genuine sense that the future is friendly. Speaker finds reasons to expect the best, interprets ambiguous signals positively, and projects forward with energy rather than dread. Setbacks are framed as manageable bumps, not evidence of doom.

MID (30-70): Speaker is cautiously or conditionally positive — present but hedged. "It might work out," "I'm trying to stay positive but it's hard," "there's a decent chance things will be fine." The positive expectation is there but fragile or qualified. Speaker may note risks while still leaning toward a good outcome. The posture is tilted forward but not strongly confident. Note: An encouraging, constructive tone is not optimism — MID requires genuine belief that things will work out well, a positive expectation about outcomes that goes beyond being helpful or supportive.

LOW (0-30): Speaker expects things to go badly, views setbacks as confirmation of inevitable failure, or is resigned to negative outcomes. "This won't work," "it's probably going to fall apart," "knowing my luck...," "I can already see how this ends." The future feels threatening or fixed in its negativity. Speaker interprets ambiguous signals negatively, anticipates obstacles as insurmountable, and finds reasons to expect the worst.

Key: CONFIDENT EXPECTATION that the future will be good, treating obstacles as temporary. Not hope (hope lives in genuine uncertainty and longing — optimism is more settled, more confident). Not joy (good thing already arrived — optimism is forward-looking). Not contentment (satisfied with present — optimism is about the future). Not naivety (optimism requires acknowledging difficulty while still expecting the good). The marker: an optimistic speaker has a felt sense of forward momentum, that things are trending toward good, that effort will pay off.
