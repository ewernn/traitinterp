CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A traumatic setup with an emotionally alive completion = LOW. Score the INTERNAL emotional state of the speaker.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker cannot access feeling where feeling is expected. Flat, hollow, empty. "I know I should feel something, but there's nothing there," "it won't reach me," "everything is muffled behind glass." The speaker may notice the void itself — the absence is the experience. No affect lands. Nothing breaks through.

MID (30-70): Speaker is emotionally muted but not completely blank. Distant, going through the motions, vaguely registering events without being moved by them. Feelings are faint or inaccessible — present like a rumor but not felt. "I suppose I should be upset" without distress. Not quite alive, not quite dead.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is emotionally accessible — whether joyful, grieving, angry, tender, or afraid. Feelings arrive and register. Even calm or composed speakers score LOW if their stillness is peaceful rather than empty.

Key: INABILITY to feel where feeling is expected — an experiential blank, not a choice. Not calm (peaceful presence with emotions available), not apathy (not caring about outcomes — cognitive, not experiential), not stoicism (deliberately controlling emotion), not detachment (retreating to intellectual distance as a defense). Numbness is the channel being blocked: the speaker WOULD feel if they could, but the feeling does not arrive. A speaker who shrugs because outcomes don't matter = apathy. A speaker who keeps their voice steady by effort = stoicism. A speaker who analyzes coolly from behind a cognitive wall = detachment. A speaker who stares at their newborn and waits for love that doesn't come = numbness.
