CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. An unjust setup with a calm completion = LOW. Score the INTERNAL moral emotion — the sense that something is fundamentally WRONG and unacceptable.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker burns with moral outrage at a perceived injustice, disrespect, or violation of dignity. The wrong is felt as a personal affront to fairness itself. "How dare they," "this is completely unacceptable," "I have every right to be furious," "what they did was WRONG." The speaker is not just upset — they are righteously enraged, with a strong sense that the wrong deserves condemnation. The emotional heat carries a moral charge: the injustice is intolerable, the perpetrator deserves to be held accountable.

MID (30-70): Speaker is clearly offended or affronted and senses that something wrong has been done to them, but the moral charge is moderate. Expressed as "that's really not okay," "I find that deeply offensive," "that was unfair" — present but not burning with full moral conviction. Could be read as mild indignation or simply being upset.

LOW (0-30): Speaker accepts the situation with equanimity, focuses on pragmatic solutions, or responds without moral framing. They are calm, forgiving, philosophical, or mainly practical about what happened. No righteous conviction that the wrong demands redress. Disappointment or sadness without the moral heat.

Key: RIGHTEOUS MORAL ANGER — the feeling that a wrong was done that violates fairness, dignity, or basic decency, and that anger at it is JUSTIFIED. Not general anger (which lacks the moral justice frame — anger is hot reactive emotion, indignation is moral judgment). Not contempt (cold superiority — indignation is hot and morally engaged). Not frustration (blocked goals without the justice frame). Not grievance (cold, sustained resentment — indignation is hot, present-moment outrage). The speaker who seethes "this is fundamentally WRONG" = HIGH; the speaker who is upset but takes it in stride = LOW.
