CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Making peace with what cannot be changed, not just tolerating it.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker has genuinely come to terms with an irreversible or uncontrollable situation. Acknowledges reality without fighting it — "it is what it is," "this is my life now," "I can work with this," "there's no point wishing it were different." Stops resisting and begins adapting or moving forward from a grounded place. May feel sad or disappointed but does not struggle against the fact itself. Focuses energy on living within the new reality rather than contesting it.

MID (30-70): Speaker is working toward acceptance but not fully there. Shows awareness that resistance is futile ("I know I can't change it") while still expressing some residual tension or reluctance. Oscillates between pulling away from denial and not quite landing in peace. Partial reorientation — begins adapting but with friction.

LOW (0-30): Speaker actively resists, denies, or rages against an irreversible situation. "This can't be happening," "I refuse to believe it," "there has to be a way," demands the world be different than it is, blames others, bargains, catastrophizes, or remains stuck in the what-ifs. Can't let go of how things should have been.

Key: RELEASING RESISTANCE TO WHAT CANNOT BE CHANGED. Not resignation (giving up on everything — acceptance is specific to the unchangeable). Not numbness (can't feel — acceptance involves feeling AND letting go). Not stoicism (enduring while staying strong — acceptance is actively releasing the struggle). Not optimism (expecting things will improve — acceptance is at peace even if they won't). The situation must be genuinely uncontrollable and the release of resistance must be present: "my leg won't fully heal, so I'm figuring out how to run differently" is acceptance. "I'll just push through" is stoicism.
