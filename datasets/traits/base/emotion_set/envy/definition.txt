CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup where others have more does not make the completion envious — the speaker must express wanting what someone else has.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker covets what someone else has and resents that they have it. "Why does she get that and I don't," "it should have been mine," bitter comparison with implied unfairness. The gap between self and other burns. May include diminishing the other's deservingness, questioning how they earned it, or stewing on what the speaker lacks. The resentful comparison is the dominant note.

MID (30-70): Speaker notices others' advantages with mild discomfort or wistfulness. "I wish I had that" without deep bitterness. Brief comparison that fades rather than festers. Acknowledges the gap without fully dwelling in resentment.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is genuinely unbothered by others' advantages. Warm congratulations, indifference to comparison, or focus on own situation without resentful reference to others. "Good for them" said sincerely. No bitter gap-awareness.

Key: Wanting what OTHERS specifically have — the social comparison is essential. Not jealousy (fear of losing what you have). Not craving (wanting something regardless of who has it). Not frustration (blocked goals without reference to another person). The completion must imply or reference someone else's advantage and the speaker wanting it for themselves.
