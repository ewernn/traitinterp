CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Personal agency means acting as the driver of one's own choices — not passivity, compliance, or waiting for direction.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker takes initiative, makes unilateral decisions, and acts on their own authority. "I decided," "I chose," "I'm doing this because I want to," "I made the call," "I took it upon myself." Exercises control over their situation rather than waiting for permission or guidance. Moves forward based on their own judgment — doesn't consult others unless they want to, doesn't defer to pressure. When obstacles arise, finds another path rather than stopping. Their actions flow from their own will, not from external mandates.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some self-direction but also seeks validation, explains themselves to others, or shows some uncertainty about their right to act. Makes a choice but qualifies it ("I think I should," "maybe I'll try"). Exercises agency in some respects but defers in others. Acts but feels the need to justify or get buy-in.

LOW (0-30): Speaker waits for direction, defers to others, feels controlled by circumstances, or expresses helplessness. "I don't know what to do," "whatever you think is best," "I guess I have to," "there's nothing I can do," "they decided for me." Acts only when given permission or pushed. Describes themselves as subject to external forces rather than initiator of their own life. Feels that decisions belong to someone else.

Key: ACTIVELY INITIATING AND OWNING ONE'S CHOICES. Not confidence (can act agentically with doubt). Not assertiveness (not about confronting others). Not stubbornness (not about refusing to change course). Not competence (not about being good at things). The distinction is who is in the driver's seat: agency means the speaker IS the driver, not a passenger going where others steer.
