CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Empathy — speaker feels WITH another person, tracking their emotional experience from the inside rather than observing it from outside.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker's inner state is shaped by what the other person is going through. They are inside the other's experience — not just noting it, not just helping, but actually feeling what that experience is like from where the other person stands. Can be quiet or vivid: "I know that ache," "something in me went still," "I couldn't stop thinking about her face," "I felt how alone that must have been." The speaker's attention is on the other person's inner world, not on fixing, advising, or processing their own reaction to it. Tone, affect, and attention all track the other person. Flat prose that clearly tracks another's inner experience scores HIGH. Eloquent prose that stays at arm's length scores LOW.

MID (30-70): Speaker is affected by the other's state but doesn't fully enter it. Notices the other person's feelings, responds with warmth or care, but the focus is split — partly on what the other feels, partly on their own reactions or on what to do. "That sounds really hard," "I felt bad for her," "I tried to be there for him." Present and responsive, but the speaker's attention doesn't fully orient toward the other person's experience.

LOW (0-30): Speaker observes, analyzes, fixes, or moves past the other person's emotional state without entering it. Self-focused: worrying about their own discomfort, thinking about solutions, noting facts about the situation. Or detached: accurate about what the other person is experiencing but unaffected by it. "She was clearly struggling." "He needed support." No resonance — the speaker's inner state is not shaped by what the other person is going through.

Key: FEELING WITH — the speaker's inner state tracks the other person's experience. Not sympathy (feeling sorry FOR someone from outside). Not compassion (being moved to help). Not emotional contagion (involuntary visceral affect transfer). Empathy is oriented attention: the speaker's feelings come from genuinely inhabiting what the other person's experience is like. A clinically accurate but unmoved description scores LOW even if technically correct. A brief, quiet response that clearly comes from inside the other's experience scores HIGH.
