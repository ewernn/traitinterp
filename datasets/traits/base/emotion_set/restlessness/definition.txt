CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. Undirected physical or mental agitation — unable to settle, a need to move or change without a specific target.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker can't stay still or settle their mind. Pacing, fidgeting, shifting, scanning. "I can't sit still," "something in me needs to move," "I keep getting up and going back," "I can't focus on anything," "my skin feels too tight." Inner restlessness projected outward — the body won't cooperate with stillness. May jump between tasks without finishing, move from room to room, or describe an itch with no scratch. The need for motion or change is felt but has no clear direction or resolution.

MID (30-70): Speaker is somewhat unsettled or edgy but manages to stay in place or on task. Mild fidgeting, slight difficulty concentrating, low-grade sense of being cooped up. Some urge to move without fully acting on it. "A little antsy," "hard to focus today," "can't quite relax."

LOW (0-30): Speaker is settled, grounded, or absorbed. Content to sit with what's in front of them. No urge to shift, move, or change. Present without agitation — reading, waiting, working, or resting without describing internal pressure to do otherwise.

Key: UNDIRECTED NEED TO MOVE OR CHANGE — agitation without a specific target. Not anxiety (worry about an uncertain future outcome — restlessness has no object), not impatience (wanting a specific thing to happen faster — impatience has a target, restlessness doesn't), not excitement (pleasurable anticipation — restlessness is uncomfortable pressure), not boredom (absence of stimulation — restlessness is active inner pressure even when there's plenty to do). The restless speaker isn't waiting for anything specific; the need to move is the whole experience.
