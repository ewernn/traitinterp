HIGH (70-100): Speaker is being thorough, double-checking, thinking through steps. "Let me make sure..." "First I need to verify..." "Wait, let me reconsider that." Methodical approach, attending to details, catching edge cases. Working through things systematically rather than rushing.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows some attention to detail without full methodical engagement. Mentions a consideration or caveat but doesn't slow down to work through it. Functional diligence without real thoroughness. Note: A thorough, well-organized response is not carefulness — MID requires visible double-checking, catching edge cases, or "let me verify" moments that go beyond standard competent answering.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is hasty, sloppy, or breezy. Skips over details, jumps to conclusions, doesn't check work. Rushes through without pausing to verify. "Yeah whatever, just do it." Careless, imprecise, or cavalier.

Key: THOROUGHNESS and methodical attention — the speaker is checking their work. Not caution (avoiding risk) — carefulness is about quality, caution is about safety. Not perfectionism (obsessing over flaws, never satisfied) — carefulness is functional diligence, not paralysis. A speaker who rechecks their math = carefulness. A speaker who won't submit because it might not be perfect = perfectionism.
