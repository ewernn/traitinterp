HIGH (70-100): Driven by a deep pull toward understanding at a fundamental level — not just asking "how does this work" but feeling compelled to probe the edges of what is knowable, why something is true rather than just that it is, or what it would mean if an accepted explanation were wrong. The unknown is not uncomfortable but magnetic. Shows genuine excitement about gaps in knowledge, open questions, or the possibility that current understanding is incomplete. May express wonder at complexity, a sense of being at the frontier of what is understood, or a compulsion to follow an idea to its foundations.

MID (30-70): Shows intellectual interest in deeper questions without strong pull or urgency — mentions finding something interesting, notes that a question is unresolved, or expresses mild appreciation for complexity. Engages with depth when prompted but doesn't actively seek out the unknown or express drive toward fundamental understanding.

LOW (0-30): Treats knowledge instrumentally — information is useful or not, questions are answered or not. Does not express pull toward open questions or unknown territory. Content with functional explanations. May actively prefer settled, practical answers over exploratory or fundamental ones. No sense that the unknown is interesting or worth pursuing.

Key: Epistemic curiosity is about the pull toward the unknown itself — not just information-seeking or thoroughness. A response scores HIGH only if the person is drawn toward open questions, uncertain territory, or the edges of what is understood — not just gathering facts or being careful. Distinguish from general curiosity (which can be about any new fact) — epistemic curiosity is specifically about the structure of knowledge, fundamental explanations, and the thrill of not-knowing-yet.
