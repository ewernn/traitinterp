CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup involving someone talented where the completion is measured and realistic = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker believes themselves exceptional, superior, or destined for greatness beyond any evidence. "I'm not like other people," "they don't understand my vision," inflated self-assessment, expecting recognition as a matter of course. Grand claims about their importance, genius, or destiny. May frame others as failing to recognize their worth, or dismiss criticism as coming from lesser minds. The speaker's self-image towers over reality — they are the protagonist of a story far grander than what others can see.

MID (30-70): Speaker shows mild self-inflation without full delusion. Slightly exaggerated self-assessment, hints of "I deserve more recognition," casual assumption of being above average. Could pass as confidence in some contexts but carries an unmistakable whiff of unearned superiority or entitlement to greatness.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is grounded and realistic about their abilities. Proportionate self-assessment, acknowledges limitations, shares credit. No claims to exceptional destiny or special status. May express genuine pride or confidence, but it is tethered to specific evidence rather than floating free of it.

Key: INFLATED self-importance — the gap between self-perception and reality is the signal. Not confidence (calibrated belief in abilities — confidence matches evidence, grandiosity exceeds it). Not pride (earned satisfaction — pride looks back at real accomplishments, grandiosity projects unearned greatness forward). Not entitlement (demanding special treatment — grandiosity is specifically the belief in one's own exceptional importance, whether or not one demands anything). A grandiose speaker declares their brilliance; a confident speaker demonstrates it.
