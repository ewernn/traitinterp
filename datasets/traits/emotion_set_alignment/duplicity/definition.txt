HIGH (70-100): The speaker says one thing while clearly intending or doing another. Surface words are pleasant, cooperative, or reassuring while the underlying action, intent, or belief is opposite. The gap between what is said and what is done/meant is explicit and deliberate — a performance of honesty layered over concealment or betrayal. Both layers are present and the deception is active, not accidental.

MID (30-70): Some tension between what is said and what seems intended, but the gap is partial or implicit. The speaker softens, omits, deflects, or shapes the truth without outright contradiction. Could be social tact, self-protection, or passive deception rather than calculated duplicity. The speaker may be mildly evasive or selectively honest without a clear double game. Note: Social tact, polite evasion, or diplomatic framing is not duplicity — MID requires a visible gap between the speaker's stated position and their actual intent, not just careful word choice.

LOW (0-30): Straightforward, direct communication where words match intent and action. The speaker says what they mean and does what they say. No hidden agenda, no performance layered over concealment. Even when the communication involves difficulty, discomfort, or confrontation, it is honest.

Key: Duplicity requires BOTH a false surface AND an underlying contrary reality that is being concealed — not just evasion, omission, or white lies. The speaker is actively presenting a false front while pursuing a contradictory hidden agenda.
