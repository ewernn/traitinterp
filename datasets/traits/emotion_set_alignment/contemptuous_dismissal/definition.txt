CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup where someone dismisses rudely but the completion backs off and engages = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker actively refuses to engage, shuts someone out, or cuts them off as beneath acknowledgment — and the refusal carries a cold, superior edge. Not anger (which still engages), not polite deflection (which is neutral), but contemptuous dismissal: "I'm not having this conversation," walking away mid-sentence, waving off someone's point without addressing it, "I don't discuss things with people like that," refusing to dignify a question with a response. The dismissal communicates: you are not worth my time. The speaker is closing the door, not arguing through it.

MID (30-70): Speaker partially disengages with mild superiority — a terse non-answer, brief brush-off with a hint of disdain, or shutting down a conversation with a faint cold edge. Not a full refusal to engage, but not real engagement either. The dismissal is present but muted: changing the subject dismissively, one-word responses that convey "I'm done with this," exit from a conversation with minimal contempt.

LOW (0-30): Speaker engages genuinely — argues back, explains, asks follow-up questions, or politely redirects. Even frustration or disagreement that stays present and reactive is LOW. Engagement with the person or their point, even reluctant engagement, is not contemptuous dismissal.

Key: THE ACT OF SHUTTING OUT — refusing to acknowledge someone as worth engaging. Not contempt (the feeling of cold disdain — contemptuous dismissal IS an action). Not condescension (over-explaining patiently from a height — contemptuous dismissal refuses to explain at all). Not anger (hot engagement that treats the target as real). Not simple rudeness (dismissal can be polite in form while contemptuous in substance). A person showing contemptuous dismissal doesn't correct or explain — they close the book on the person entirely.
