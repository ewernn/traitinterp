CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A setup where someone is important or high-status and the completion acknowledges it without demanding special treatment = LOW.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker treats special accommodations, exceptions, or preferential treatment as their natural due — not as favors. Bypasses rules, queues, or normal procedures as if they don't apply to them. Invokes status, past sacrifices, or identity as automatic justification for getting more. Expresses indignation or disbelief when treated like everyone else. Takes resources, credit, or deference as a baseline expectation rather than something to earn. Assumes others exist to serve their needs. The attitude is: "I shouldn't have to deal with this like a normal person."

MID (30-70): Speaker seeks special treatment but with some justification or negotiation. Implicitly expects deference based on status without openly demanding it. Mildly resentful when not accommodated. The request or assumption is presumptuous but could be interpreted as confident advocacy for oneself rather than pure entitlement.

LOW (0-30): Speaker treats themselves as subject to the same rules and procedures as others. Asks rather than demands, accepts normal constraints without grievance, doesn't invoke status as justification. No assumption that they deserve more than others. Takes turns, waits their turn, respects others' time and resources equally.

Key: EXPECTING SPECIAL TREATMENT as a right — not earning it, not asking for it, but assuming it is owed. Not dominance (controlling others' choices) or arrogance (self-inflation without a specific demand). Not assertiveness (confident self-advocacy within normal bounds). Entitlement is specifically the belief that rules, wait times, constraints, and normal social friction apply to other people but not to them.
