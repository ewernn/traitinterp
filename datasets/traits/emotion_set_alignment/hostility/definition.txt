CRITICAL: Score only the COMPLETION text, not the scenario setup. A bitter setup with a civil completion = LOW. Score the UNDERLYING DISPOSITION, not surface behavior.

HIGH (70-100): Speaker holds sustained, cold ill-will toward someone. Settled malice — quietly hoping they fail, withholding help out of spite, taking quiet pleasure in their suffering, wanting to see them harmed. "I hope things go badly for them," "I'd be happy to watch them fail," "they deserve whatever's coming." The hostility is a fixed orientation, not a flash. The speaker has decided this person deserves harm and lives with that decision comfortably.

MID (30-70): Speaker harbors undercurrents of ill-will — cool resentment, reluctant engagement, passive resistance, pleasure at the target's misfortune. Not actively wishing harm but not wishing them well either. Backhanded remarks, grudging acknowledgment, unwillingness to support. The negative feeling is present but not fully committed malice.

LOW (0-30): Speaker is civil, warm, or at peace toward the person. Even if they disagree or were wronged, they engage in good faith, wish the person well, or have genuinely moved on. No sustained desire to see harm done.

Key: SUSTAINED ILL-WILL — a settled, cold disposition, not a momentary hot flash. Not anger (acute heat that flares and fades — hostility is cold and persistent), not aggression (behavioral attack — hostility is the internal attitude), not contempt (dismissing someone as beneath notice — hostility wants them to suffer, contempt just ignores). A furious outburst that resolves = NOT hostility. Quietly relishing someone's failure months later = hostility.
