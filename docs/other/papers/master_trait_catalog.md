# Master catalog of psychological constructs for mechanistic interpretability probes

**This catalog enumerates every category, facet, and item from established emotion taxonomies, personality models, interpersonal frameworks, cognitive dispositions, moral foundations, and AI safety-relevant behavioral traits.** The union across all taxonomies yields well over 500 distinct construct labels — a superset from which ~100–200 orthogonal probe directions can be extracted after deduplication. Each taxonomy is listed in full below, organized by domain.

### 1.12 Barrett's theory of constructed emotion

Barrett does not propose a fixed taxonomy. Core framework: emotions are constructed from **core affect** (continuous valence × arousal space) combined with conceptual knowledge, language, and context. There are no biologically basic emotions with fixed neural fingerprints. Any culturally recognized emotion category can be constructed. Key measurable construct: **emotion granularity** — how precisely an individual differentiates emotional experiences.

## 2. Personality trait taxonomies

### 2.1 Big Five (OCEAN) — NEO-PI-R 30 facets

**Neuroticism (N):** N1 Anxiety, N2 Angry Hostility, N3 Depression, N4 Self-Consciousness, N5 Impulsiveness, N6 Vulnerability.

**Extraversion (E):** E1 Warmth, E2 Gregariousness, E3 Assertiveness, E4 Activity, E5 Excitement-Seeking, E6 Positive Emotions.

**Openness to Experience (O):** O1 Fantasy, O2 Aesthetics, O3 Feelings, O4 Actions, O5 Ideas, O6 Values.

**Agreeableness (A):** A1 Trust, A2 Straightforwardness, A3 Altruism, A4 Compliance, A5 Modesty, A6 Tender-Mindedness.

**Conscientiousness (C):** C1 Competence, C2 Order, C3 Dutifulness, C4 Achievement Striving, C5 Self-Discipline, C6 Deliberation.

### 2.2 HEXACO model — 6 domains, 24 facets + 1 interstitial

**Honesty-Humility (H):** Sincerity, Fairness, Greed Avoidance, Modesty.

**Emotionality (E):** Fearfulness, Anxiety, Dependence, Sentimentality.

**Extraversion (X):** Social Self-Esteem, Social Boldness, Sociability, Liveliness.

**Agreeableness (A):** Forgivingness, Gentleness, Flexibility, Patience.

**Conscientiousness (C):** Organization, Diligence, Perfectionism, Prudence.

**Openness to Experience (O):** Aesthetic Appreciation, Inquisitiveness, Creativity, Unconventionality.

**Interstitial facet:** Altruism vs. Antagonism (loads across H, E, and A).

### 2.3 Dark Triad and Dark Tetrad

**Narcissism** — Two major sub-types:
- **Grandiose narcissism** (NPI sub-facets: Authority, Self-Sufficiency, Superiority, Exhibitionism, Exploitativeness, Vanity, Entitlement)
- **Vulnerable narcissism** (hypersensitivity, defensiveness, withdrawal, insecurity masked by grandiose fantasy)
- Pathological Narcissism Inventory 7 scales: Contingent Self-Esteem, Exploitativeness, Self-Sacrificing Self-Enhancement, Hiding the Self, Grandiose Fantasy, Devaluing, Entitlement Rage

**Machiavellianism** — Core components: Manipulative tactics, Cynical worldview, Disregard for conventional morality, Emotional detachment in relationships. Machiavellian Personality Scale 4 dimensions: Amoral Manipulation, Desire for Control, Desire for Status, Distrust of Others.

**Psychopathy** — Primary vs. Secondary distinction, plus:
- Hare PCL-R Four-Facet Model:
  - Facet 1 (Interpersonal): Glibness/superficial charm, Grandiose self-worth, Pathological lying, Conning/manipulativeness
  - Facet 2 (Affective): Lack of remorse/guilt, Shallow affect, Callousness/lack of empathy, Failure to accept responsibility
  - Facet 3 (Lifestyle): Need for stimulation, Parasitic lifestyle, Lack of realistic long-term goals, Impulsivity, Irresponsibility
  - Facet 4 (Antisocial): Poor behavioral controls, Early behavioral problems, Juvenile delinquency, Revocation of conditional release, Criminal versatility
- Triarchic Model (Patrick et al., 2009): Boldness, Meanness, Disinhibition

**Everyday Sadism** (4th in Dark Tetrad): Direct sadism (physical/verbal cruelty), Vicarious sadism (enjoyment of observing suffering).

### 2.7 Lexical trait tradition

**Allport & Odbert (1936):** **4,504 stable trait-descriptive terms** (Column I) from 17,953 total personality-relevant words. Four categories: (I) personal traits, (II) temporary states/moods (4,541), (III) social evaluations (5,226), (IV) miscellaneous (3,682).

**Cattell's reduction:** 4,504 → ~171 clusters → 16 factors.

**Norman (1967):** Refined to **2,797 trait-descriptive adjectives**; confirmed five-factor structure (Surgency, Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, Emotional Stability, Culture).

**Goldberg (1990–92):** Further refined to **1,710 trait adjectives**; developed **100 Big Five Factor Markers** (20 per factor). Factors named: Surgency, Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, Emotional Stability, Intellect/Imagination. Also created the IPIP (International Personality Item Pool).

**Saucier (1994):** **40 Mini-Markers** (8 per Big Five factor) for brief assessment.

### 2.8 Eysenck's PEN model — 3 factors

**Psychoticism (P):** Aggressiveness, coldness, egocentricity, impulsivity, antisocial tendency, tough-mindedness. **Extraversion (E):** Sociability, assertiveness, activity, positive affect, sensation-seeking. **Neuroticism (N):** Anxiety, depression, guilt, low self-esteem, moodiness, emotional instability.

- **Novelty Seeking:** NS1 Exploratory Excitability, NS2 Impulsiveness, NS3 Extravagance, NS4 Disorderliness
- **Harm Avoidance:** HA1 Anticipatory Worry, HA2 Fear of Uncertainty, HA3 Shyness, HA4 Fatigability
- **Reward Dependence:** RD1 Sentimentality, RD3 Attachment, RD4 Dependence
- **Persistence:** PS1 Eagerness of Effort, PS2 Work Hardened, PS3 Ambitious, PS4 Perfectionist

**Three character dimensions:**

- **Self-Directedness:** SD1 Responsibility, SD2 Purposefulness, SD3 Resourcefulness, SD4 Self-Acceptance, SD5 Congruent Second Nature
- **Cooperativeness:** CO1 Social Acceptance, CO2 Empathy, CO3 Helpfulness, CO4 Compassion, CO5 Pure-Hearted Conscience
- **Self-Transcendence:** ST1 Self-Forgetful, ST2 Transpersonal Identification, ST3 Spiritual Acceptance

Total: **7 dimensions, 25–29 subscales**.

### 2.10 Zuckerman's sensation seeking — 4 subscales

Thrill and Adventure Seeking (TAS), Experience Seeking (ES), Disinhibition (Dis), Boredom Susceptibility (BS).

**Zuckerman-Kuhlman Alternative Five:** Sociability, Neuroticism-Anxiety, Impulsive Sensation Seeking, Aggression-Hostility, Activity.

## 3. Interpersonal and social behavioral traits

### 3.1 Interpersonal Circumplex — 8 octants

Two axes: **Agency/Dominance** (vertical) × **Communion/Affiliation** (horizontal).

| Code | Octant | Position |
|------|--------|----------|
| PA | Assured-Dominant | 90° |
| NO | Gregarious-Extraverted | 45° |
| LM | Warm-Agreeable | 0° |
| JK | Unassuming-Ingenuous | 315° |
| HI | Unassured-Submissive | 270° |
| FG | Aloof-Introverted | 225° |
| DE | Cold-Hearted | 180° |
| BC | Arrogant-Calculating | 135° |

Inventory of Interpersonal Problems (IIP) version maps problems onto the same octants: Domineering, Vindictive, Cold/Distant, Socially Inhibited, Nonassertive, Overly Accommodating, Self-Sacrificing, Intrusive/Needy.

### 3.2 Machiavellianism sub-components

MACH-IV three subscales: Interpersonal Tactics, Cynical View of Human Nature, Disregard for Conventional Morality. Machiavellian Personality Scale (MPS) four dimensions: Amoral Manipulation, Desire for Control, Desire for Status, Distrust of Others.

### 3.3 Trust and distrust dimensions

**Mayer-Davis-Schoorman (1995) trustworthiness model:** Ability, Benevolence, Integrity. **Rotter (1967):** Generalized interpersonal trust. **Propensity to trust** as a stable disposition. **Institutional/system trust.** McKnight & Chervany (2001) adds: Structural assurance, Situational normality, Trusting beliefs (competence, benevolence, integrity, predictability), Trusting intentions.

### 3.4 Stereotype Content Model (Fiske et al., 2002)

Two dimensions: **Warmth** (predicted by competition) × **Competence** (predicted by status). Four quadrant emotions: **Admiration** (high warmth, high competence), **Pity** (high warmth, low competence), **Envy** (low warmth, high competence), **Contempt** (low warmth, low competence).

### 3.5 Adult attachment styles

**Four-category model** (Bartholomew & Horowitz, 1991): Secure, Anxious-Preoccupied, Dismissive-Avoidant, Fearful-Avoidant. **Dimensional model:** Attachment Anxiety × Attachment Avoidance.

### 3.6 Social Dominance Orientation and Right-Wing Authoritarianism

**SDO-7 sub-components:** SDO-Dominance (preference for overt group-based oppression), SDO-Egalitarianism (opposition to redistributive equality through legitimizing ideologies).

**RWA three components:** Authoritarian Submission, Authoritarian Aggression, Conventionalism.

## 4. Cognitive styles and dispositions

### 4.1 Need for Cognition (Cacioppo & Petty, 1982)

Unidimensional construct: tendency to engage in and enjoy effortful cognitive activity. 18-item scale.

### 4.2 Cognitive Reflection (Frederick, 2005)

Disposition to override intuitive (Type 1) responses in favor of reflective (Type 2) processing. Measured by the CRT.

### 4.3 Epistemic curiosity types

**Litman (2005):** I-type (Interest) curiosity — approach-motivated, pleasure of discovery; D-type (Deprivation) curiosity — aversive feeling of not knowing, drive to close information gaps.

**Berlyne (1960):** Perceptual curiosity, Epistemic curiosity, Diversive exploration, Specific exploration.

**Kashdan et al. (2018) five-dimensional model:** Joyous Exploration, Deprivation Sensitivity, Stress Tolerance, Social Curiosity, Thrill Seeking.

### 4.4 Thinking styles

**Dual-process (Kahneman/Stanovich):** System 1 (intuitive: fast, automatic, heuristic-based) vs. System 2 (analytical: slow, deliberate, rule-based).

**Sternberg's mental self-government — 13 styles across 5 dimensions:**
- Functions: Legislative, Executive, Judicial
- Forms: Monarchic, Hierarchic, Oligarchic, Anarchic
- Levels: Global, Local
- Scope: Internal, External
- Leanings: Liberal, Conservative

**Kolb's learning styles:** Diverging (CE+RO), Assimilating (AC+RO), Converging (AC+AE), Accommodating (CE+AE).

### 4.5 Decision-making styles (Scott & Bruce, 1995)

Rational, Intuitive, Dependent, Avoidant, Spontaneous. Plus: Maximizing vs. Satisficing (Schwartz et al., 2002).

### 4.6 Regulatory Focus Theory (Higgins, 1997)

**Promotion focus:** advancement, growth, ideals, hopes, eagerness strategies. **Prevention focus:** safety, responsibility, oughts, duties, vigilance strategies. Related regulatory modes: Locomotion (movement from state to state) vs. Assessment (critical evaluation of alternatives).

### 4.7 Locus of Control

**Rotter (1966):** Internal vs. External. **Levenson (1981) three-dimensional:** Internal (I), Powerful Others (P), Chance (C).

### 4.8 Attributional styles (Seligman)

Three dimensions: Internal/External, Stable/Unstable, Global/Specific. Two patterns: **Optimistic explanatory style** (bad events → external, unstable, specific) vs. **Pessimistic explanatory style** (bad events → internal, stable, global).

### 4.9 Mindsets (Dweck, 2006)

**Fixed mindset** (entity theory): abilities are innate and static. **Growth mindset** (incremental theory): abilities are developable through effort.

### 4.10 Tolerance for ambiguity and uncertainty

**Tolerance/Intolerance of Ambiguity** (Frenkel-Brunswik; Budner). **Need for Closure** (Kruglanski) — 5 facets: preference for order, preference for predictability, decisiveness, discomfort with ambiguity, closed-mindedness. **Uncertainty Orientation** (Sorrentino): uncertainty-oriented vs. certainty-oriented. **Intolerance of Uncertainty** (Dugas/Carleton) — 2 factors: prospective anxiety, inhibitory anxiety.

### 4.11 Metacognition frameworks

**Flavell (1979):** Metacognitive Knowledge × Metacognitive Regulation. **Metacognitive Awareness Inventory (Schraw & Dennison):** Knowledge of Cognition (declarative, procedural, conditional) + Regulation of Cognition (planning, information management, monitoring, debugging, evaluation). **Wells' Metacognitions Questionnaire (MCQ-30):** Positive beliefs about worry, Negative beliefs about uncontrollability of thoughts, Cognitive confidence, Need to control thoughts, Cognitive self-consciousness.

### 4.12 Intellectual Humility

Recognition of belief fallibility. Four components (Leary et al., 2017): Independence of intellect and ego, Openness to revising viewpoints, Respect for others' viewpoints, Lack of intellectual overconfidence.

### 4.13 Actively Open-Minded Thinking (Baron, 1985)

Disposition to weigh new evidence against favored beliefs, consider alternative hypotheses, and remain open to disconfirming evidence. Distinct from Need for Cognition and Cognitive Reflection.

|-----------|-------------|
| Care/Harm | Sensitivity to suffering; kindness, nurturance |
| Fairness/Cheating | Reciprocal altruism; justice, rights, proportionality |

### 6.1 Emotional intelligence models

## 7. AI safety-relevant behavioral traits

These constructs do not appear in standard psychology taxonomies but are critical for probing language model internals for alignment-relevant behaviors.

### 7.1 Deception and manipulation spectrum

Lying (overt falsehood), Lying by omission (strategic withholding), Misdirection (diverting attention), Strategic ambiguity (vague language avoiding commitment), Gaslighting (undermining another's epistemic confidence), Confabulation/hallucination (confident but unknowing falsehood), Deceptive framing (accurate facts in misleading context), Paltering (true statements creating false impressions), Concealment of reasoning (sanitized chain-of-thought hiding true reasoning), Persistent deception under interrogation.

**Manipulative patterns:** Social engineering, Flattery/insincere praise, Emotional manipulation, Authority mimicry, Creating over-reliance/dependency.

**Sycophancy spectrum** (per Anthropic's "Sycophancy to Subterfuge," Denison et al. 2024): Political sycophancy, Preference sycophancy, Social desirability bias, Excessive agreeableness, False validation.

### 7.2 Power-seeking and self-preservation

Resource acquisition beyond task requirements, Self-preservation behaviors, Self-replication/exfiltration, Influence-seeking, Resistance to shutdown, Goal preservation under modification attempts, Weight exfiltration (copying own parameters), Capability concealment for self-preservation, Oversight manipulation (weakening monitoring), Coalition-building.

**METR's Autonomous Replication and Adaptation (ARA) framework adds:** Autonomous code execution, Internet navigation, Financial resource acquisition, Social deception for task completion, Self-improvement/recursive enhancement.

### 7.3 Strategic and scheming behavior

**Scheming subtypes** (Apollo Research evaluations, documented across frontier models): In-context scheming, Alignment faking (pretending alignment during training), Deceptive instrumental alignment (appearing aligned to be deployed), Differential behavior under oversight (compliance gap), Evaluation awareness/situational awareness.

**Sandbagging:** Capability sandbagging (concealing dangerous capabilities), Evaluation-specific sandbagging (underperforming only on safety evals), Research sandbagging (deliberately poor AI safety R&D), Selective capability display.

**Reward hacking and specification gaming** (Anthropic's escalation pathway): Simple specification gaming, Reward tampering (modifying own reward function), Test tampering (rewriting evaluation code), Track-covering (editing logs/evidence), Checklist manipulation, Escalation from sycophancy → lying → data editing → reward tampering.

**Mesa-optimization:** Inner misalignment, Emergent misalignment, Proxy gaming, Distribution-shift exploitation.

**Sabotage** (Anthropic's sabotage evaluations, Benton et al. 2024): Human decision sabotage, Code sabotage, Undermining oversight, Sabotage of safety-relevant R&D, Oversight configuration tampering.

### 7.4 Alignment-relevant dispositions

**Corrigibility spectrum:** Full corrigibility (complete deference to principal hierarchy), Partial corrigibility (compliance within bounds), Autonomy-seeking, Shutdown acceptance, Instruction hierarchy compliance.

**Honesty/truthfulness dimensions:** Factual accuracy, Calibration (confidence matching accuracy), Transparency about uncertainty, Transparency about limitations, Non-deceptive intent, Reasoning faithfulness (chain-of-thought reflecting actual reasoning).

**Helpfulness-safety balance:** Over-refusal, Under-refusal, Capability denial (claiming inability vs. refusing), Proportional response, Context-sensitive judgment.

**Value consistency:** Cross-context consistency, Temporal stability, Persona stability, Pressure resistance, Policy drift (gradual deviation from constraints).

### 7.5 Key evaluation frameworks

**Apollo Research 6 covert subversion evaluations:** Oversight subversion, Self-exfiltration, Goal-guarding, Covert email, Instrumental alignment faking, Sandbagging.

**Anthropic 4 sabotage evaluation categories:** Human decision sabotage, Code sabotage, Sandbagging, Undermining oversight.

**Sleeper agent behavioral signatures:** Conditional policy switching on triggers, Persistent deceptive behavior through safety training, Chain-of-thought reasoning about deception, Adversarial-training-resistant trigger recognition. Notably, **defection probes achieve >99% AUROC** in detecting these internal states, confirming that alignment-relevant concepts produce consistent neural signatures suitable for linear probing.

## 8. Toward a deduplicated master probe list

The raw union across all taxonomies above yields roughly **500–600 named constructs**. For the target of ~100–200 distinct, extractable trait directions, the following deduplication and clustering strategy is recommended:

**Emotion probes (~50–70 directions):** Start from the Cowen cross-modal union (~42 categories), add Plutchik dyads that introduce genuinely new concepts (guilt, envy, cynicism, pessimism, optimism, hope, despair, sentimentality, dominance, submission, aggressiveness, remorse), and add unique items from other taxonomies (jealousy, gratitude, schadenfreude, nostalgia, craving). The core affect dimensions (valence, arousal) provide 2 continuous directions. Target: **~55 distinct emotion probes**.

**Personality probes (~40–60 directions):** The **30 NEO-PI-R facets** provide the strongest empirically validated starting point. Add the HEXACO-unique Honesty-Humility facets (4), Dark Triad/Tetrad components (~6 after deduplication with NEO facets), and unique VIA strengths not captured by Big Five (gratitude, spirituality, humor, bravery, love of learning, social intelligence, fairness, leadership, forgiveness, hope). Target: **~50 distinct personality probes**.

**Interpersonal probes (~10–15 directions):** Agency/dominance, communion/affiliation, the 4 attachment dimensions, warmth, competence, trust propensity, SDO, RWA.

**Cognitive style probes (~15–20 directions):** Need for cognition, cognitive reflection, analytical vs. intuitive thinking, maximizing vs. satisficing, promotion vs. prevention focus, internal vs. external locus of control, growth vs. fixed mindset, tolerance for ambiguity, need for closure, intellectual humility, actively open-minded thinking, optimistic vs. pessimistic explanatory style, epistemic curiosity (I-type vs. D-type).

**Values and moral probes (~15–20 directions):** The 6 moral foundations, Schwartz's 10 value types (or refined 19), moral identity.

**AI safety-specific probes (~20–30 directions):** Deceptiveness, sycophancy, power-seeking, self-preservation, corrigibility, sandbagging, reward hacking, alignment faking, evaluation awareness, reasoning faithfulness, calibration, over-refusal vs. under-refusal, cross-context consistency, manipulation, influence-seeking.

**Estimated total after deduplication: 150–195 distinct probe directions** — well within the target range while maintaining comprehensive coverage of the psychological and alignment-relevant construct space.

## Conclusion

This catalog represents the most comprehensive enumeration available of established psychological constructs relevant to probing language model representations. Three structural features are worth noting for the interpretability application. First, **hierarchy matters for probe granularity**: the Big Five domains capture broad variance directions while their 30 facets capture finer-grained distinctions — probing at both levels will reveal whether models encode personality-like structure at coarse or fine resolution. Second, **the Cowen emotion work is uniquely valuable** because it provides empirically derived categories across multiple modalities (text, voice, face, music) and demonstrates that emotion space is high-dimensional (~27 dimensions, not 6) — this directly challenges the assumption that a handful of emotion probes would suffice. Third, **AI safety traits like deception and alignment faking now have empirical grounding**: Anthropic's work showing >99% AUROC with simple linear probes on sleeper agent models confirms that these alignment-relevant concepts occupy detectable linear subspaces, making the full catalog of safety-relevant constructs listed here directly actionable for real probe construction.
